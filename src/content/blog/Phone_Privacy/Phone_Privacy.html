Introduction

Phones are one of the most vulnerable devices we carry around. Most likely, your phone is actually a glorified surveillance device that you carry around with you 24/7. It is essential that you secure your phone if you want to maintain privacy on the internet, free of tracking.

Isn't stock android secure enough?

If you update android on a regular basis, it may be secure enough, however, security is not privacy. You can have a secure phone and still have your data leaked to the likes of google, facebook, or even amazon. Most likely, if you care about your privacy and you just want something that works, consider:

Apple

Yes, it's one of the big corporations that can and will still collect your information. However, it is actually far less invasive than stock android phones made by samsung and other providers. They're also pretty serious about security too; however, the way they go about this isn't too ideal. Almost all their apps in their ecosystem still collect information about you, but they only send some of it, and only to Apple. The bad part about this is that many of the apps are closed source, so if you're a FOSS advocate like me, a better option is:

GrapheneOS

In my opinion, this, LineageOS, and CalyxOS are the only three ways you're going to get any privacy at all with phones. Unfortuantely, if you're unknowledgeable with phones, the best thing you're going to get is an iphone, because i imagine that installing a custom ROM on an android phone would be pretty hard for those types of people.

The OS that I use personally is GrapheneOS. If you install this operating system, you will have to remove google from your life forever, due to GAPPs not working with grapheneOS, so BE WARNED! Removing google from your phone entirely is the only way to get privacy out of your phone, however, so if you do actually want true privacy, just keep that in mind.

GrapheneOS is actually very usable, if you don't rely on google services. Its user interface is like that of stock android, and you can customize it very easily. Installing F-droid will provide you with a suite of free and open source applications. Speaking of applications:

Applications

So you've got yourself a privacy and security hardened operating system. Now what?

Newpipe

Tired of going on the web version of youtube and getting tracked on their website? Newpipe is a heavily underrated application that allows you to browse youtube, without the frontend tracking and fingerprinting. Note that youtube still has your ip and browsing habits when you use newpipe, so be sure to spoof your ip using either a trusted vpn, or your own trust free vpn. This app allows you to see and download videos, and although the comments break sometimes, it can still on occassion display them properly. I use this app as my main youtube frontend.

andOTP

Tired of using google authentication for all of your two factor authentication accounts? AndOTP is an open source implementation of two factor authentication, and can store accounts and generate new authentication codes. These apps can be found in F-droid.

Conclusion

Phones are not designed for privacy, security, or anonimity. They are designed to track you, and in order to stop that tracking completely, you need to step outside of your comfort zone and basically get rid of google. If you're not willing to do this, just get an IPhone or something.
